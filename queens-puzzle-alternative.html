How the Star Battle Alternative Works

On the Daily Logic Lab board, the aim is to place one star in every row, one star in every column, and one star in every color region. A star also blocks the eight cells around it. That diagonal rule is what makes Star Battle feel different from many Queens variants.

The best solving rhythm is to mark impossible cells, find the most constrained row or region, place a forced star, and then collect the new eliminations created by that star. If you enjoy narrowing down candidates in Queens, this will feel familiar.

When Star Battle Is the Better Fit

Star Battle is a good fit when you want a puzzle that is short, logical, and easy to play on a phone. It is also useful if you want unlimited practice instead of waiting for the next daily Queens board. Because the rules are compact, each round can be played in a few minutes, but the no-touch rule still gives the grid enough bite.

How to Switch From Queens to Star Battle

The fastest adjustment is to treat every placed star as both an answer and a blocker. In many Queens puzzles, a placed piece mainly finishes its row, column, or region. In Star Battle, it also blocks the neighboring cells around it. That means one move can remove a cluster of nearby candidates.

If you are coming from Queens, keep your usual scan pattern, but add one extra question: "What does this star touch?" Marking those surrounding cells right away makes the puzzle feel much clearer and prevents most illegal placements.
