Two Not Touch Puzzle

Two Not Touch Puzzle Online

A two-not-touch puzzle is a placement puzzle where pieces cannot touch each other. Daily Logic Lab uses the Star Battle version: place stars on a grid so every row, column, and color region has a star, while no two stars touch.

The rule sounds small, but it drives most of the solving. A single star blocks its neighboring cells and can force new moves across the board.

What Does Two Not Touch Mean?

Two placed pieces are not allowed to share an edge or a corner. In Star Battle terms, no two stars may be horizontal neighbors, vertical neighbors, or diagonal neighbors. If a star sits in the middle of the board, the eight cells around it are immediately impossible.

How This Version Works

  • One star in every row.
  • One star in every column.
  • One star in every color region.
  • No touching stars, including corners.
  • Every generated board is solver-verified for a unique solution.

Two Not Touch vs Star Battle

Beginner Solving Tips

Start by marking cells around any star you place. Then check whether a row, column, or region has only one legal cell left. That is the most common forced move in a two-not-touch puzzle.

If nothing is forced, look for a region whose candidates all sit in the same row or column. That row or column may be reserved by the region, which can remove candidates elsewhere. This is the step that often separates a clean solve from random guessing.

Worked Example

Suppose a star is placed in the center of a small area. The cells directly above, below, left, and right are now impossible. The four diagonal cells are impossible too. If one of those cells was the last candidate for another region, that region must look elsewhere.

This is why the no-touch rule is powerful: one confirmed star does more than fill one square. It reshapes the candidate map around it.

Common Mistakes

  • Forgetting that corners count as touching.
  • Placing a star before checking whether its row or column already has one.
  • Only scanning rows and columns while ignoring color regions.
  • Leaving neighboring cells unmarked after a star is placed.

The safest habit is to mark first and solve second. Each impossible cell you mark makes the remaining legal cells easier to see, and the puzzle usually opens up without guessing.

FAQ

What does two not touch mean?

It means no two placed pieces can touch by an edge or a corner.

Is this the same as Star Battle?

It is closely related. Daily Logic Lab uses a Star Battle board where the no-touch rule is one of the main constraints.

Can stars touch diagonally?

No. Diagonal touching is forbidden.
