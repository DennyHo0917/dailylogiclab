Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 18, 2026

Daily Logic Lab provides free browser-based logic puzzles and puzzle helper tools. This policy explains what information may be collected when you use the site.

Information You Provide

Daily Logic Lab does not currently require accounts, sign-ins, or payment details. If you contact the site through GitHub issues, the information you choose to post there is handled by GitHub and may be public.

Local Browser Storage

The puzzle experience may store small pieces of data in your browser, such as streak status, best times, solved dates, hint counts, and recent in-browser event records. This data helps the game remember your local progress. You can clear it by clearing site data in your browser settings.

Analytics

Daily Logic Lab uses Google Analytics to understand aggregate site usage, such as page views, puzzle starts, hints, checks, and general device or browser information. Analytics helps improve the puzzle experience and identify pages that need better explanations.

Google may use cookies or similar technologies for measurement. You can learn more in Google's privacy documentation at policies.google.com/privacy.

Advertising

Daily Logic Lab does not currently display advertising. If advertising is added later, the site may use Google AdSense or related Google advertising services. Google and its partners may use cookies to serve, measure, and personalize ads based on your visits to this and other websites.

Third-party vendors, including Google, may use cookies to serve ads based on prior visits to Daily Logic Lab or other websites. Google's advertising cookies enable Google and its partners to serve ads based on visits to this site and other sites on the internet.

You can control personalized advertising through Google's ad settings at adssettings.google.com.

Third-Party Links

The site may link to external pages, such as GitHub or Google documentation. Daily Logic Lab is not responsible for the privacy practices of external websites.

Children's Privacy

Daily Logic Lab is a general-audience puzzle site and does not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through a linked contact channel, please use the Contact page.

Changes to This Policy

This policy may be updated as the site adds features, analytics changes, or advertising support. The updated date at the top of this page will reflect material changes.
