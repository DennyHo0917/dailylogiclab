Star Battle Puzzle

Star Battle Puzzle Rules and Online Practice

Star Battle is a grid logic puzzle about placing stars under four simple constraints: every row needs a star, every column needs a star, every outlined or colored region needs a star, and stars cannot touch one another, even diagonally.

Star Battle Rules

  • Place exactly one star in each row.
  • Place exactly one star in each column.
  • Place exactly one star in each color region.
  • Stars may not touch horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
  • Every shown board should be solvable by logic, not guessing.

How to Play

  1. Scan the rows, columns, and regions for groups with very few possible star cells.
  2. Mark cells that cannot hold a star because they touch another star or belong to a solved group.
  3. Place a star when a row, column, or region has exactly one legal cell left.
  4. After placing a star, remove all cells touching it and the rest of its row, column, and region.
  5. Repeat until every row, column, and region has its star.

What Makes Star Battle Different?

Star Battle Rows, columns, regions, and no touching stars. Diagonal neighbors are forbidden.

Two Not Touch Pieces cannot touch each other. Often another name for the same no-touch idea.

Beginner Strategy

Start with the smallest or most constrained color regions. If a region has only one square that can still hold a star, that square is forced. Once a star is placed, it eliminates its row, column, region, and all touching squares.

When a move is not obvious, count legal cells. Empty cells are not automatically legal. A cell is illegal if a star already exists in its row, column, or region, or if it touches a star. This simple distinction solves many beginner mistakes.

Worked Example

Imagine a region has three empty cells. One touches a star, so it is illegal. One is in a row that already has a star, so it is illegal too. The third cell is the only legal position left, so the region forces a star there.

After placing that star, mark all eight neighboring squares as impossible. Then mark the rest of the row, column, and region. Those eliminations often create another forced move in a nearby group.

FAQ

What is the goal of a Star Battle puzzle?

Place the required stars so every row, column, and region is satisfied, while no stars touch each other.

Can stars touch diagonally?

No. Diagonal touching is forbidden, just like horizontal and vertical touching.

Do I need to guess?

A well-checked Star Battle puzzle should have a logical path. Look for the group with the fewest legal cells before guessing.

Is this the same as Queens?

It is related. Both are placement puzzles, but Star Battle emphasizes regions and the no-touch rule.
